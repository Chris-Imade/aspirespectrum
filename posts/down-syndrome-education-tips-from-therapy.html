Down Syndrome Education – Tips from Therapy

  • Home
  • Resources
  • Article

Published Aug 2025 • 8–10 min read

Children with Down syndrome bring unique strengths to learning—warmth, persistence, and a strong desire for connection. Therapy-informed strategies can help families and educators support speech, language, literacy, and independence across home and school routines.

Build Communication Foundations
  • Model multi-modal communication: Pair speech with signs, pictures, and gestures to support understanding and expression.
  • Focus on functional language: Practice words and phrases useful in daily routines (e.g., "help," "more," "finished").
  • Repetition with variety: Repeat targets across different activities to support generalization.
Strengthen Speech Intelligibility
  • Precision practice: Use visuals and mirrors for sound placement; combine drills with motivating play.
  • Rate and breath support: Model slower pacing and short phrases to improve clarity.
  • Celebrate approximations: Reinforce efforts and gradually shape toward the target sound or word.
Literacy & Learning
  • Rich print environment: Label key areas at home/school; read predictable books with repeated lines.
  • Phonological awareness: Play with rhymes, syllables, and initial sounds during songs and games.
  • Write for meaning: Use name writing, simple journals, and choice-making with pictures/words.
Executive Function & Independence
  • Visual supports: First–then boards, checklists, and calendars for transitions and planning.
  • Task chaining: Break tasks into steps; fade prompts as independence grows.
  • Choice and agency: Offer structured choices to build self-advocacy and engagement.
Partnering with Your Team

Collaboration among caregivers, educators, SLPs, OTs, and other providers ensures consistency. Share goals, agree on a few strategies to emphasize, and check progress together. Small, steady steps add up.

Getting Started

If you’re building a plan for your child or student, consider a short list of priorities for the next 4–6 weeks, choose daily routines to embed practice, and use simple data (tallies or checkmarks) to celebrate growth.
