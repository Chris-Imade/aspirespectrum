Local Earth Day Activities for Children

  • Home
  • Resources
  • Article

Published Apr 2025 • 5–7 min read

Earth Day is a great opportunity to get outside, connect with your community, and practice communication and daily living skills. With a little preparation and visual supports, these activities can be fun, calm, and meaningful for all learners.

Community Clean-Up
  • Pack gloves, a small trash grabber, and clear rules. Use a simple first–then visual ("pick up 5, then playground").
  • Model requesting help and labeling items ("paper," "plastic," "heavy").
  • Count collected items together to add math and turn-taking.
Planting and Gardening
  • Create a picture sequence: dig, place seed, cover, water.
  • Offer choices (seeds, tools) to encourage communication and autonomy.
  • Build a routine—water at the same time daily to practice planning.
Nature Scavenger Hunt
  • Use a visual checklist with pictures (leaf, rock, bird, flower).
  • Practice requesting ("I want binoculars"), commenting ("I see red flower"), and waiting turns.
  • Celebrate each find with a sticker or stamp.
Reduce–Reuse Crafts
  • Make simple crafts from clean containers—plan steps and gather materials together.
  • Embed fine-motor practice (cut, glue, stick) and safety rules.
  • Take photos to make a mini book retelling the activity.
Preparation Tips
  • Preview with a short social story or photos of the location.
  • Pack snacks, water, headphones, and a fidget kit.
  • Choose quieter times of day if your child is sensitive to crowds.

With simple visuals and predictable routines, Earth Day can be fun and skill-building. For personalized supports and visuals, contact Aspire Spectrum.
