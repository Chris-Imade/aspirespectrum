Resource Guide for Speech Therapists

  • Home
  • Resources
  • Article

Published Aug 2025 • 7–9 min read

This guide gathers therapy tools, evidence-based strategies, and home practice ideas to support learners at every stage of communication. Use it to streamline planning and align teams around functional goals.

Assessment & Goal Setting
  • Collect caregiver priorities and strength-based histories.
  • Use dynamic assessment and observation across routines.
  • Write measurable, functional goals that matter in daily life.
Helpful Tools & Activities
  • Low-tech AAC: Core vocabulary boards, single-message buttons, choice boards.
  • Language kits: Pretend play sets, picture books, open-ended toys for modeling.
  • Articulation: Minimal pairs, mirror work, tactile cues; mix drills with games.
  • Visual supports: Schedules, first–then boards, token systems for motivation.
  • Home practice: Short “practice in the routine” cards for mealtime, bath, play.
Collaboration That Works

Progress accelerates when caregivers and educators use the same strategies. Share quick tip sheets, model strategies during sessions, and celebrate small wins to maintain momentum.

Data & Progress Monitoring
  • Track a few key measures weekly (e.g., spontaneous requests, MLU, accuracy by sound).
  • Use simple visuals to share progress with families and set next-step targets together.
Conclusion

Effective therapy blends play, visuals, and caregiver coaching with clear, functional goals. The right tools help communication skills grow where it matters most—at home, school, and in the community. For customized recommendations and therapy support, contact Aspire Spectrum.
