Why Do Some Kids Need Speech Therapy?

  • Home
  • Resources
  • Article

Published Aug 2025 • 6–8 min read

At Aspire Spectrum, speech and language therapy helps children build essential communication skills. Children may benefit from speech therapy for a variety of reasons, including delayed speech, articulation challenges, expressive/receptive language delays, fluency concerns, or social communication needs related to Autism Spectrum Disorder and other developmental differences.

Evidence-based approaches—such as play-based interventions, modeling, visual supports, naturalistic developmental behavioral interventions (NDBI), and caregiver coaching—empower families to support progress at home and in the community.

Why Speech Therapy?

Speech-language pathologists (SLPs) do far more than help with pronunciation. Therapy can target vocabulary, sentence structure, social communication (pragmatics), following directions, narrative skills, and augmentative & alternative communication (AAC). The goal is functional communication that improves a child’s daily life.

Common Reasons for Referral
  • Limited vocabulary, late talking, or difficulty combining words
  • Articulation and phonological sound errors affecting intelligibility
  • Receptive language delays (understanding questions, concepts, or directions)
  • Expressive language delays (putting thoughts into words and sentences)
  • Fluency concerns such as stuttering
  • Social communication challenges (gestures, turn-taking, eye contact, conversation)
What Evidence-Based Therapy Looks Like

Therapy is tailored to the child’s interests and needs. Sessions may include play routines, books, songs, and everyday items to model meaningful language. Visual supports, choice boards, and routines help generalize skills. We collaborate with caregivers to embed strategies into mealtime, bath time, and play.

  • Play-based learning: Motivating activities increase engagement and practice opportunities.
  • Modeling & expansion: Adults model slightly longer phrases to scaffold growth.
  • Visual supports: Pictures, signs, and schedules make language concrete.
  • Caregiver coaching: Brief, focused strategies families can use daily.
Getting Started

The process typically includes an intake conversation, evaluation, collaborative goal setting, and a therapy plan. Frequency and duration vary based on the child’s profile and family priorities. Progress is reviewed regularly and goals are adjusted as skills develop.

Tips for Caregivers
  • Follow your child’s lead—talk about what they find interesting.
  • Model short, clear phrases in daily routines; repeat and expand on their attempts.
  • Use gestures and visuals to support understanding.
  • Celebrate small wins and make communication fun.
Every child learns differently. A personalized plan created by a qualified therapist can make communication more effective and confident.

If you’re wondering whether your child may benefit from speech therapy, we’re here to help. Contact Aspire Spectrum to learn more or schedule an evaluation.
